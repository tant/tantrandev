## What to Do Next: My Recommendations

Now that the foundational elements of your website are in place, the next phase focuses on content creation and strategic promotion to truly establish your authority and attract opportunities. Here are my recommendations, in order of priority:

### 1. Write and Publish Your First Playbook (Highest Priority)

This is the single most impactful action you can take to elevate your personal brand. It moves your website beyond a static profile to a dynamic platform that provides genuine value.

*   **Why it's critical:**
    *   **Prove Your Expertise:** It demonstrates your knowledge and experience in a tangible way.
    *   **Build Trust & Authority:** Offering high-value content establishes you as a thought leader.
    *   **Attract Your Audience:** Useful content is shareable and improves your SEO, drawing organic traffic.
*   **Actionable Steps:**
    1.  **Choose Your Topic:** Select one of the playbook ideas we discussed (e.g., "Scaling a Tech Team," "P&L Ownership for Engineers," or "GTM Strategy for B2B Tech"). Pick the one you are most passionate and knowledgeable about first.
    2.  **Outline the Content:** Structure your playbook with clear sections: introduction, problem statement, your principles/approach, actionable steps, and a conclusion.
    3.  **Write the First Draft:** Focus on getting your ideas down. Don't aim for perfection initially; you can refine it later. The goal is to provide detailed, practical, and genuinely helpful insights.
*   **How I can help:** I can assist with structuring your playbook, reviewing drafts for clarity and impact, and designing any necessary visuals.

### 2. Gather and Add Testimonials

Authentic endorsements from others significantly boost your credibility.

*   **Why it's important:** Testimonials provide powerful social proof, offering third-party validation of your skills and impact. They add a personal and relatable dimension to your professional profile.
*   **Actionable Steps:**
    *   **Identify Key Contacts:** Reach out to 3-5 former colleagues, clients, or mentors who can speak to your strengths as a manager and leader.
    *   **Request Specific Feedback:** Instead of a generic request, ask them to highlight a specific achievement, a quality they admire, or the impact you had on a project or their career.
*   **How I can help:** Once you collect these, I can design and implement a dedicated "Testimonials" section on your homepage or "About" page.

### 3. Enhance Your "About" Page with a Personal Video

Video content creates a deeper, more immediate connection with your audience.

*   **Why it's a game-changer:** A short video allows visitors to see and hear you, building rapport and trust much faster than text alone. It humanizes your brand.
*   **Actionable Steps:**
    *   **Record a Short Video (60-90 seconds):** Use your smartphone or webcam. Focus on authenticity over high production value.
    *   **Content Idea:** Briefly introduce yourself, reiterate your core "I Build, I Lead" philosophy, and perhaps touch upon your "lazy triathlete" metaphor to show personality.
*   **How I can help:** I can integrate this video seamlessly into your "About" page.

### 4. Develop a Content Strategy and Calendar

Consistency is key to long-term brand building.

*   **Why it's important:** A planned approach ensures a steady stream of valuable content, keeps your audience engaged, and reinforces your expertise over time.
*   **Actionable Steps:**
    *   **Brainstorm Topics:** Generate a list of potential subjects for future playbooks, blog posts, or short articles.
    *   **Create a Simple Calendar:** Plan when you intend to publish new content. Even a quarterly plan is a great start.
*   **How I can help:** I can assist you in brainstorming topics and setting up a basic content calendar.

### My Strongest Recommendation

**Focus on writing and publishing your first playbook.** This will be the most impactful step in establishing your authority and providing tangible value to your audience. Once that is complete, we can leverage it for promotion and then move on to the other recommendations.
